MAPLE FLOW

Maple Flow is a live campus transit web application we built during Hack OHI/O 2025 after realizing how frustrating campus transportation can be including things like long waiting, overcrowded buses, and unpredictable routes. It was built as part of the Honda × 99P Labs Friction Finder Challenge, and the goal was simple: make it easier for students to get where they’re going with less stress. Our team designed Maple Flow to show real-time bus locations, predict crowd levels, and help users decide whether to prioritize speed or comfort for each trip. Overall, working on this project gave me hands-on experience building a full-stack application under tight time constraints, collaborating closely in a fast-paced team, and turning a common student frustration into a practical, user-focused solution.
